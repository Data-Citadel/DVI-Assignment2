# Calculate number of obs per group & median to position labels
medians = df.groupby(['quartiles'])['Tweets'].median().values
nobs = df['quartiles'].value_counts().values
nobs = [str(x) for x in nobs.tolist()]
nobs = ["Tw: " + i for i in nobs]
 
# Add it to the plot
pos = range(len(nobs))
for tick,label in zip(pos,ax.get_xticklabels()):
    ax.text(pos[tick], medians[tick] + 0.03, nobs[tick],
            horizontalalignment='center', size='x-small', color='w', weight='semibold', fontsize=14)

ax=sns.stripplot(x="quartiles", y="Tweets", data=df, color="black",jitter=0.2, size=4.5)
plt.show()

Inferences¶

It is found that the tweets with keyword #DVICourse has the range of counts from (10, 95) which will also be the min and max of the given data. After visualizing the data in a Boxplot, we could observe few key aspects of the data. The observations are as follows,

  1. 35% of tweets are from 1st quartile i.e, between 10 to 25,
  2. 25% are from 2nd quartile i.e, between 26 to 50,
  3. 23% from 3rd quartile 51 to 75 and
  4. 16% from 4th quartile 76 to 95
  5. Median line falls around 55. Could see that 60 % of the tweets falls below 2nd quartile.

From the observations we could infer that the 1st quartile has more number of data instances but the frequencies of tweets are in lower range (10-22), whereas the 4th quartile has lesser instances but the frequecies of the tweets are at higher range (76-95)
